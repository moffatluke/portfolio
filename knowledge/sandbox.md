# Sandbox (Startup Accelerator)
Luke is part of Sandbox, BYU-Idaho's student startup accelerator. Sandbox is
where he builds real products on a team and looks for problems worth solving.

## What Sandbox is
Sandbox is an immersive, two-semester startup accelerator where students build
and launch a real software company from scratch while earning college credit.
Students work in small teams, typically 2 to 4 people made up of a software
engineer, a product manager, and a designer. Participants can earn up to 18
college credits while building their startup, so they make progress on their
degree while doing it.

## Background and reach
The Sandbox program started at BYU through the Rollins Center for
Entrepreneurship and Technology and has since expanded to other universities,
including BYU-Idaho, where Luke participates. It now runs across roughly nine
universities in five states.

## Track record
The Sandbox program has a strong track record. Companies that came out of it and
raised outside funding are collectively valued at around $149 million, six
Sandbox companies have been accepted into Y Combinator (one of the most
prestigious startup accelerators in the world), and Sandbox alumni average about
25% higher starting salaries than peers who didn't do the program.

## Why it fits Luke
Sandbox reflects how Luke likes to work: shipping useful software that solves
real problems, working on a team, and treating projects as products rather than
demos.
